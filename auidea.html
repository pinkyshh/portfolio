What is the app 'AuIdea' about?

AuIdea is a simple application where students will be shown a list of top n projects generated using a recommendation algorithm. These projects are curated based on their courses studied throughout the semesters.

  • Development: Jun - Oct 2021
  • App Type: Android
  • Language: Kotlin
  • Platform: Android Studio (Development) & Adobe XD (Prototype)

Design Process & Research

Our team knew from firsthand experience that VMS students have difficulty choosing themes for senior projects. We intended to address these issues and find a solution that was acceptable to both students and faculty mentors.


  • Research
  • Brainstorming
  • User Persona and Journey
  • Wireframing and Prototyping
  • User Testing
  • Usability Testing


One-on-one interviews with students from the VMS faculty were performed to learn more about the various challenges they were having or new features they might want to see implemented. We were able to empathize with each user and understand their greatest pain point areas as a result of our research.

Student’s greatest pain points:
- No place to gain information: Don’t know where to find information
- No reference: Can’t figure out what kind of projects to make
- Clueless: Don’t know on what basis to choose a project or advisor

It encouraged us to think outside the box and expand our possibilities for improving our ideas and meeting the demands of our users. We were able to set our project's goals and objectives by referring to the three key user pain points.

Our Solution:
- Recommend projects based on skills
- A showcase of senior project
- Display professor’s information (project advisor)

By referring to the problem statements identified during user research, our team began brainstorming possibilities for our application. We then made affinity diagrams, in which we grouped all comparable traits together and labeled them.


User Persona

We made up fictional characters to reflect a subset of our target demographic and their activities. We based one character from each program because VMS offers two undergraduate programs, Information Technology and Computer Science, and we wanted to give a true depiction of the students.

User Journey

We developed a narrative, which tells the story of how the user interacts with our app. This was accomplished by mapping the story based on our user research and defining scenarios for each persona and their expectations.


The content on the interfaces was organized using gestalt concepts in this step to make it aesthetically beautiful and easy to grasp.

Wireframe

It was evident from the affinity diagram which features we would incorporate in our application. As a result, we produced our first prototype. It was a monochrome portrayal that focused mostly on the app's structure and how the user would traverse it.

Prototype

We produced computer-based high-fidelity prototypes with realistic (mouse-keyboard) user interactions. Before we started developing, it got us as close as possible to a good depiction of our application and its user interface. The theme and color scheme of the screens were now based on the faculty's brand color.


The user testing for AuIdea was done with faculty students. We tried to analyze user behavior as they interacted with our software throughout the process. 'If you were looking for all of the professor's information, where would you expect to find it?' is an example question. The layout of our program matched majority of the user's replies to such questions. Following the testing process, the user was asked a series of questions to learn more about how they felt while attempting to use the app and if there was anything they would change. The software was subsequently converted into an Android Mobile App, with all of the UI programmed and turned into functional displays.


We chose to undertake user experience testing with real VMS Faculty members in order to test the interface for usability. Students were requested that specific tasks be completed, such as finding the profile screen, filtering a specific project from the home screen, and so on. They were also asked some basic task-related feedback questions, such as whether anything surprised them or if they performed as predicted.
